Weight Loss

In weight loss, the fundamental principle is achieving a caloric deficit. This means burning more
calories than you consume. While this can be achieved most effectively through diet and exercise,
choose nutrient-dense foods,and create a sustainable caloric deficit. In short, calculate your BMR
(Basal Metabolic Rate), depending on exercise frequenzy and lever of exercise height, age, and weight.

Workout Routine

Squat, Deadlift, Benchpress and Military press. These 4 is basically the base and from there you build further
an important key is correct technique in weightlifting, you should start off with vety low weight and focus

Later on when you know the exercises you start with proggressive overload which means you go to failure
Remember for building muscle you first (tear them down) after recovery from your muscle sore you start build.
I give this simple day a week split, it's important to remeber doing some cardio which seems easy to skip for most.

Weekly Workout Routine

Day 1 - Full Body

Squats:

3 sets x 12 reps

Push-Ups:

3 sets x 10 reps

Military press:

3 sets x 10 reps

Bent-Over Rows:

3 sets x 12 reps

Plank:

3 sets, hold for 30 seconds each

Day 2 - Cardiovascular Exercise

Brisk walking, jogging, or cycling for 30 minutes

Day 3 - Full Body

Lunges:

3 sets x 10 reps per leg

Benchpress or with dumbbells:

3 sets x 12 reps

Lat Pulldowns:

3 sets x 12 reps

Deadlift:

3 sets x 10 reps
